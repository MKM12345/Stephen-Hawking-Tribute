Stephen Hawking

1942-2018

I was just a young boy of seven when I discovered black holes and Stephen Hawking's theory of Hawking radiation. I was already into space, but after learning about this, it sparked a wonderful curiosity about black holes and made me want to learn these brain-boggling phenomena of space.

Leadership in General:

  • Courage in the Face of Adversity: Despite being diagnosed with ALS (Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis, is a disease that harms the nerves in your brain and spine, causing muscles to weaken and eventually stop working.) at the age of 21, Hawking continued his scientific pursuits, showcasing incredible resilience.
  • Intellectual Brilliance: His groundbreaking work in theoretical physics, especially on black holes, demonstrated unparalleled intellect.
  • Science Communication: Hawking had a gift for explaining complex scientific concepts in a way that captivated and inspired people worldwide.
  • Perseverance: Overcoming physical limitations, he persevered to contribute significantly to the understanding of the cosmos.
  • Humanitarianism: Hawking used his platform to address important issues, advocating for scientific advancement and raising awareness about the environment.

Why I Was Inspired:

Not only did it help me be interested in black holes, Stephen Hawking's life and work served as a profound inspiration to me. His ability to overcome physical challenges, his intellectual brilliance, and his dedication to science and humanity continue to motivate and shape my own aspirations.

"Remember to look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious. And however difficult life may seem, there is always something you can do and succeed at. It matters that you don't just give up."

Stephen Hawking
